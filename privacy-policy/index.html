Privacy Policy

Last updated: 10 July 2026

This policy explains how Henry Oliver, trading as Olivers Consulting (“we”, “us”, “our”), collects and uses personal data. We are the data controller for the information described here.

Who we are

Henry Oliver, trading as Olivers Consulting, a sole trader based in London.

  • Address: 3 Yeoman Street, London, SE8 5DF
  • Email: henry@oliversconsulting.co.uk
  • ICO registration number: ICO Registration in Progress

What this website does

Our website (oliversconsulting.co.uk) is an information page. It does not collect personal data through forms and does not run analytics, advertising, or behavioural tracking.

We offer an optional Google Fonts setting so the site can appear in its intended typefaces. Google Fonts are not loaded unless you actively allow them through the privacy banner. The booking link takes you to the separate Cal.eu website.

The personal data we collect

We only hold personal data that you actively give us, apart from the optional technical data sent directly to Google if you allow Google Fonts:

  • If you email us: your name, email address, and anything you choose to include in your message, such as details about your business.
  • If you book a call: the name, email address, details you enter into the scheduling tool, and the meeting time.
  • If you become a client: the contact and business information needed to carry out the work and invoice you.
  • If you allow Google Fonts: your browser connects directly to Google. Google may receive your IP address and technical request information such as browser details and the page making the request. We do not receive or use that request data.

We do not collect special category data, and we do not knowingly collect data about anyone under 18.

Why we use it, and our lawful basis

  • To respond to your enquiry and provide services you have asked about. Lawful basis: performance of a contract, or steps taken at your request before entering a contract.
  • To send you a proposal, invoice, or follow up on our conversation. Lawful basis: legitimate interests in running and following up on our business, or performance of a contract once we are working together.
  • To meet legal and tax obligations, such as keeping records for HMRC. Lawful basis: legal obligation.
  • To load Google Fonts. Lawful basis: your consent. The fonts are not requested from Google unless you select Allow Google Fonts. You can withdraw your consent at any time through the website’s Privacy settings button.

Booking a call

Bookings are handled by Cal.eu, which acts as a processor on our behalf. When you book, the details you enter are processed through its platform. Cal.eu’s own privacy terms govern its handling of that data. We only receive the booking details you submit.

Who we share data with

We do not sell your data or share it for marketing. We use a small number of service providers:

  • Cal.eu — call scheduling.
  • Google Workspace — email and document storage.
  • Google Fonts — optional font delivery, only after you consent. The font request is made directly by your browser to Google.

Where a provider stores or processes data outside the UK, the provider is responsible for applying appropriate safeguards, such as an adequacy decision or UK-approved contractual protections.

How long we keep it

  • Enquiries that do not become work: up to 12 months, then deleted.
  • Client records: for the duration of the work and for 6 years afterwards, to meet tax and accounting requirements.
  • Your Google Fonts choice: stored in your browser until you change it or clear the site’s browser data. We do not keep a server-side copy.
  • Google Fonts request data: controlled by Google under its own privacy terms. We do not receive or retain it.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict use of your personal data, to object to processing, and to data portability. To exercise any of these, email henry@oliversconsulting.co.uk. We will respond within one month.

You can withdraw consent for Google Fonts at any time by selecting Privacy settings and choosing Continue without.

You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office (ico.org.uk) if you believe your data has been mishandled, though we would appreciate the chance to put things right first.

Changes

We may update this policy. The current version is always the one published at oliversconsulting.co.uk with the date shown above.
